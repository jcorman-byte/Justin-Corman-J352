NBA Offense at a Glance: Early-Season Leaders

Using ESPN data to highlight which players are driving their teams’ scoring and playmaking from October 21st to November 21st.

By Justin Corman

November 21, 2025

The first month of the NBA season always brings breakout performances and eye-popping box scores. To see who is really carrying their team on offense, this project looks at early-season leaders in both points per game and assists per game.

Using game-by-game statistics from ESPN.com for contests played between October 21st and November 21st, I pulled the top 10 scorers and top 10 passers in the league. The charts below show how tightly packed the race is at the top and which stars show up on both lists.

Chart 1: NBA Points Per Game Leaders title
Luka Doncic leads all scorers at 34.6 points per game, with Tyrese Maxey and Shai Gilgeous-Alexander close behind as part of a group of stars hovering around the 30-point mark.

The difference between first and tenth is just over seven points per game, which shows how many players are close to scoring 30 a night. Doncic sets the pace, but Maxey, Gilgeous-Alexander, Antetokounmpo and others are close enough that one hot week could shuffle the rankings.

Chart 2: NBA Assists Per Game Leaders
Nikola Jokić tops the league at 11.1 assists per game, with Cade Cunningham and Josh Giddey anchoring a group of primary playmakers who create shots for everyone else.

Unlike the scoring chart, the assists leaderboard is dominated by primary ball-handlers and playmaking bigs. Jokić separates himself as a center who runs Denver’s entire offense, while guards like Cunningham and Giddey show how much responsibility young creators carry for their teams.
